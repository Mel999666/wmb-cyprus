How these results are calculated
  • Each judge scores every band in four categories:
    • Musicianship (40%)
    • Originality (25%)
    • Material quality (20%)
    • Stage or online presence (15%)
  • These percentages are combined into a single readiness score for each band per judge.
  • Step 1: Normalizing scores — Judges vary in how strict or generous they are, so we also convert scores into ranks. Each judge’s favourite band gets rank 1, the next gets rank 2, and so on. This ensures every judge’s opinion carries equal weight.
  • Step 2: Combining ranks across judges — From these per-judge rankings we calculate:
    • Median rank (the middle value) — main measure of shared consensus.
    • Average rank — used as a secondary measure of consistency.
    • Average readiness — the weighted score based on the four categories above.
  • Lower ranks indicate stronger overall placement; higher readiness means stronger overall performance.
  • Step 3: Guardrails — Two additional checks ensure both consensus and standout passion are respected:
    • Consensus rule: If a band appears in the top 6 of at least three judges, they outrank bands that do not.
    • Passion rule: If a band is ranked 1st or 2nd by at least three judges, they are guaranteed a top-6 position.
  • Step 4: Final order — Bands are ordered using the following hierarchy:
    • Average readiness (highest first)
    • Median rank (used as a tie-breaker)
    • Average rank (secondary tie-breaker)
    • Guardrail eligibility (if triggered)
  • This method puts overall readiness at the centre, while still balancing collective agreement and standout performances, and keeping the process transparent and fair.
